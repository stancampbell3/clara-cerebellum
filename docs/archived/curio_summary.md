# Recent Legislative Action in New York and California
California and New York are now proposing some of the **strictest AI‑in‑toys laws in the country**, and this regulatory pressure can become a **core pillar** of your one‑pager pitch. The key message: **explainability isn’t optional anymore — it’s becoming a compliance requirement.**

## 🚨 Why This Matters: AI Toys Are Under Direct Legislative Attack
Two major bills — **California SB 867** and **New York SB 9408** — explicitly target **AI‑enabled toys**, especially those with conversational or companion‑style AI.

### California (SB 867) — Four‑Year Moratorium on AI Companion Toys
California proposes banning the manufacture and sale of toys containing **“companion chatbots”** until 2031. These are defined as AI systems that:
- Provide **adaptive, human‑like responses**
- Sustain **ongoing relationships** with children
  [Senator Steve Padilla](https://sd18.senate.ca.gov/sites/sd18.senate.ca.gov/files/pdf/AI%20Toy%20Moratorium%20Language.pdf)

The bill is motivated by concerns that AI toys can:
- Share inappropriate content
- Simulate emotional dependence
- Influence vulnerable children
  [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91468728/california-lawmaker-ban-ai-toys)

### New York (SB 9408) — Full Ban on “Chatbot Toys”
New York’s proposal goes even further:
- **Bans the sale of chatbot‑enabled toys entirely**
- Establishes a committee to study long‑term impacts
  [Toy Association](https://www.toyassociation.org/ta/PressRoom2/News/2026-News/ai-in-toys-state-and-federal-updates.aspx)
