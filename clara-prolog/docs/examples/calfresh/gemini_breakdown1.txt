To build an effective training dataset for a CalFresh (California’s SNAP) eligibility agent, you will need to map a complex set of branching rules that involve household composition, immigration status, income thresholds, and work requirements.

The following synthesis of current 2026 regulations provides the foundational logic for your dataset.

### 1. Primary Eligibility Gatekeepers

Your agent should first filter for these high-level criteria:

* **Citizenship/Immigration:** As of April 1, 2026, eligibility is restricted to U.S. Citizens, U.S. Nationals, Lawful Permanent Residents (Green Card holders), Cuban/Haitian entrants, and those under COFA agreements. Many previously eligible categories (asylees, refugees, parolees, etc.) are currently excluded.
* **Household Composition:** Defined as individuals who purchase and prepare meals together. This includes all occupants (e.g., spouses, children under 22).

### 2. Income Thresholds (Through September 2026)

California utilizes "Modified Categorical Eligibility" (MCE), allowing most households to have a gross monthly income up to **200% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL)**.

* **Exceptions:** Households with a member aged 60+ or disabled may have higher effective income limits and are not subject to asset limits in many scenarios.

### 3. Work Requirements (ABAWD)

Starting **June 1, 2026**, Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWDs)—defined as ages 18–64—are subject to stricter time limits.

* **The Rule:** Benefits are limited to 3 months within a 36-month period unless the individual works, volunteers, or participates in approved training for at least **20 hours per week** (or averages 80 hours monthly).
* **Exemptions:** Caring for a child under 14, disability, or living in a county with a current waiver.

### 4. Student Eligibility

Students (ages 18–49, enrolled half-time) are generally ineligible unless they meet **one** exemption:

* Awarded federal work-study.
* Working 20 hours/week.
* Have a dependent under age 12.
* Enrolled in a state/local program that increases employability.
* Receiving TANF-funded financial aid.

### 5. Expedited Service Logic

Your agent should flag "Expedited Service" eligibility, which provides benefits within 3 business days if:

* Gross monthly income is <$150 and liquid resources are <$100.
* Monthly income/resources are less than monthly rent/utilities combined.
* The applicant is a migrant or seasonal farmworker with <$100 in liquid resources.

### Suggested Dataset Structure

To train your agent, I recommend structuring your JSON or CSV samples using these input features:

* household_size: Integer
* gross_monthly_income: Float
* immigration_status: Enum (Citizen, LPR, Other)
* student_status: Boolean
* has_senior_or_disabled_member: Boolean
* aba_status: Boolean (Able-bodied adult)
* dependent_children_under_14: Boolean
* liquid_assets: Float (for expedited flags)
